Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026 · App: BabyCam · Developer: Zuhaib Ali Shah

In short: BabyCam turns two of your own phones into a baby monitor. Live video and audio go directly between your two devices, end-to-end encrypted — we never see, upload, or store them. There are no accounts and no sign-up, and we never sell your data. We use Google Firebase for anonymous usage analytics and crash diagnostics to keep the app reliable.

1. Who we are

BabyCam is an independent, free app developed by Zuhaib Ali Shah. For any privacy question or request, contact mz.ashah@outlook.com.

2. Live camera, microphone and audio/video

  • The baby device captures camera and microphone; the parent device receives them. Streaming is peer-to-peer (WebRTC) and encrypted in transit (DTLS-SRTP).
  • Your audio and video are never uploaded to us or stored on any server. They exist only live, on your two devices, during a session.
  • Camera, microphone and playback all start OFF — the parent explicitly turns them on. When no audio is being sent, the microphone hardware is released (so the OS privacy indicator only shows while talk/listen is actually active).
  • Any snapshots or video clips you choose to save are written only to your own device's local gallery/storage — never to us.

3. Two-way talk, remote control, lullabies & alerts

  • Two-way talk sends the parent's microphone audio to the baby device (same encrypted P2P path). Remote controls (toggle the baby's camera/mic/flashlight, flip camera, play a lullaby or a chime, trigger the low-battery alert) are short encrypted control messages — they carry no media and nothing is stored by us.
  • Lullabies / white-noise are generated on the baby device; no audio files are uploaded.

4. Pairing & connection data

  • Devices pair with a short 4-digit code shown on the baby device and typed on the parent device. A random 128-bit room secret is also exchanged so the code alone can never decrypt your media. Pairing information is stored only on your devices.
  • To help the two phones find each other, small connection-setup ("signaling") messages pass through a third-party message broker (HiveMQ). These messages are additionally AES-256-GCM encrypted by the app, so the broker only ever relays ciphertext. They contain no audio or video.
  • When the two phones are on different networks, media may be relayed through a TURN server (Cloudflare, with Metered as a fallback) purely to traverse firewalls. Relayed media stays end-to-end encrypted; the relay cannot read it.

5. Remote wake (push)

If the baby device has been backgrounded or put to sleep, the parent can wake it. This uses a Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) push, relayed by a small stateless Cloudflare Worker. The baby device's push token is shared only with your paired parent device (through the encrypted pairing) and is used only to wake the baby app — it never reveals your media.

6. Nearby Talk (optional walkie-talkie)

BabyCam includes an optional push-to-talk feature that is completely separate from baby monitoring. It can connect over Bluetooth, the same Wi-Fi, or the internet with a channel code.

  • Bluetooth is used only to discover nearby BabyCam devices by a fixed service identifier — we never derive, request, or store your location.
  • The internet channel is encrypted using the channel code. Voice is half-duplex (push-to-talk) and nothing is recorded or stored — audio exists only while you hold the button.
  • This feature can be turned off remotely by the developer and is not required to use the monitor.

7. Safety reminders & app lock

  • Safety reminders (e.g. bedtime, feeding) are scheduled and stored only on your device; no reminder content leaves the phone.
  • The optional app lock (PIN or fingerprint/biometric) is verified entirely on your device. Your PIN is stored only as a salted, hashed value; biometrics never leave the device's secure hardware.

8. In-app updates

BabyCam can check for a newer version and install it for you. To do this it fetches a small public version file and, if you choose to update, downloads the new app file from our public releases page on GitHub. This check sends no personal data — it is a plain download, like opening a web link.

11. Third-party services

For transparency, BabyCam relies on these services purely for connectivity and diagnostics — none of them receive your video or audio:

  • HiveMQ — relays encrypted connection-setup messages (ciphertext only).
  • Cloudflare — TURN relay for cross-network connections, and a small worker that forwards wake pushes.
  • Google Firebase / Cloud Messaging — analytics, crash reporting, remote configuration, and wake pushes.
  • Google Analytics — anonymous visit/download counts for this website only.

12. Data retention & deletion

  • Audio/video is never retained — it is not stored anywhere.
  • Pairing data, reminders, and settings live on your device; clearing them ("Forget pairing") or uninstalling the app removes them.
  • Anonymous analytics/crash data held by Firebase can be deleted on request — email mz.ashah@outlook.com and we will action deletion.

13. Children's privacy

BabyCam is a tool for parents and guardians. It is not directed at children as users and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The camera simply serves the caregiver.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves; material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
